Hello

I'm Shachi Solanki

I am a computer science student at the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign and will be graduating in December, 2019. I truly believe that technology has the power to change lives and make the world a better place.

I have a passion for teaching and believe everyone deserves access to an education. My love for teaching began my senior year of high school when I acted as a student intern to help teach AP Computer Science at my high school. My freshman year of college I acted as a course assistant for CS 125, Introduction to Computer Science, at my university. Throughout the summer of 2017, I had to priviledge of being a teaching assistant for the Girls Who Code Summer Immersion Program.

I am an adovcate for getting more women into STEM fields. The gender gap is an issue that is very near to my heart, and I am constantly working to close it through my continuious involvement with Girls Who Code, Women in Engineering, and Women in Computer Science.

Outside of tech, my interests include reading, baking, and drinking lots of coffee!

Girls Who Code

I joined Girls Who Code in May 2017 as a Summer Immersion Program teaching assistant and it changed my life. My involement with the organization allowed me to restore my confidence in my own abilities, and fed my hunger to be a part of the movement to bridge the gender gap in technology. I had the opportunity to teach a subject that I love, establish a network of people that supported my ambitions, and get to know 19 incredible young women that will change the world through technology. I continue my involvement with Girls Who Code as a volunteer student facillitator of the Girls Who Code Club at UIUC.
